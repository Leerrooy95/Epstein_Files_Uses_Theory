## Part II: The Saudi Significance of 1789

### Historical Context

The year 1789 was pivotal for the First Saudi State (Emirate of Diriyah, 1744-1818).

**Key Event**: In 1789, Prince Saud bin Abdulaziz led Saudi-Wahhabi forces to conquer the Al-Hasa region (Eastern Arabia), defeating the Bani Khalid tribe.

**Why This Mattered**:
1. **Economic Access**: Gave the Saudi state access to the Persian Gulf coast and trade routes
2. **Strategic Depth**: Transformed a local Najdi emirate into a regional power
3. **Religious Expansion**: Enabled spread of Wahhabi doctrine into Shi'a-majority eastern areas
4. **Foundation for Expansion**: Set the stage for capture of Mecca (1803) and Medina (1805)

Prince Saud later became known as "Saud the Great" (Saud Al Kabeer) and ruled 1803-1814.

**Sources**: Wikipedia "Saud bin Abdulaziz Al Saud"; Saudipedia "First Saudi State"; Wikipedia "Conquest of al-Hasa"
