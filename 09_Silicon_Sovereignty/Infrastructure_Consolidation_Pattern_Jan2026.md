- Niantic Spatial ($250M funding): Spinoff company owns 3D geospatial mapping technology, AR infrastructure, Visual Positioning System (VPS)
- Scopely maintains $50M investment in Niantic Spatial (data access maintained)

**The Niantic Structure and Surveillance Implications**:

*Understanding the Split*:
- **Niantic Gaming Division** (Scopely/Saudi PIF): Owns games (Pokémon Go, Monster Hunter Now, etc.)
- **Niantic Spatial** (Independent): Owns 3D mapping infrastructure and geospatial AI
- **Critical Connection**: Scopely's $50M investment in Niantic Spatial maintains access

*How the "Mapping Loop" Works*:
1. **Game Incentive**: App prompts players to scan specific locations for in-game rewards
2. **User Action**: Players walk around landmarks, filming from multiple angles
3. **Data Upload**: Video/sensor data automatically transmitted to build 3D map
4. **Result**: High-resolution geospatial intelligence gathered at zero cost

*The Control Vector*:
- Whoever controls the **game** controls the **scanning incentives**
- Games can be updated to spawn "rare items" at any location
- Players organically flock to those locations with cameras
- Data collection occurs without users recognizing intelligence gathering

*Surveillance Capability Example*:
- **Scenario**: Entity wants 3D scan of critical infrastructure (power station, government facility, protest location)
- **Method**: Update game to spawn high-value rewards at target location
- **Execution**: Thousands of players arrive, scan location from all angles with phones
- **Outcome**: Comprehensive 3D mapping without deploying intelligence assets

*Why the Structure Matters*:
- Saudi PIF (via Savvy/Scopely) owns the **behavioral incentive layer** (games)
- Maintains financial access to **mapping infrastructure** (Niantic Spatial) through $50M investment
- Can potentially direct where data collection occurs without technically "owning" the map
- Structure may circumvent CFIUS foreign ownership concerns while maintaining functional control

**Historical Data Collection**:
- Pokémon Go: 30 billion miles of location tracking data
- Global coverage of public spaces, landmarks, infrastructure
- Real-time movement patterns from 100M+ users
- 3D environmental scans from AR gameplay

**Significance**: 
- Behavioral data from gaming patterns
- Location data with unprecedented granularity
- 3D world mapping capabilities updated in real-time
- Potential for directed intelligence collection through game mechanics
- eSports competition infrastructure provides additional behavioral/engagement data

### Physical Infrastructure Bottleneck

**Understanding Data Center Security Tiers**:
- **Tier 1-3 Data Centers**: Commercial-grade facilities with standard power and security
- **Switch (Tier 5 Platinum)**: Military-grade infrastructure with independent power grids, earthquake/plane crash resistance, zero roof penetrations (preventing water damage and aerial access)
- **Client Base**: Pentagon, intelligence agencies, major financial institutions requiring highest security

**January 26, 2026**: SoftBank halts talks to acquire Switch data centers  
**Deal Value**: ~$50 billion  
**Switch's Role**: Las Vegas-based company that invented "Tier 5 Platinum" standard for ultra-secure data centers

**Why Switch Matters for Stargate**:
- Military-grade AI infrastructure requires fortress-level physical security
- Standard data centers inadequate for sensitive government AI operations
- Switch facilities designed to survive natural disasters and physical attacks
- Zero-penetration design prevents surveillance/intrusion vectors

**Deal Failure Implications**: 
- SoftBank pursued Switch to provide physical data centers for Stargate
- Without Switch, Stargate lacks military-grade physical infrastructure
- Forces reliance on less secure alternatives (see: Project Colossus issues below)
- Capital and compute secured, but physical infrastructure remains constrained
- Security/speed tradeoff: Using rapid-deployment but lower-security facilities

**Alternative Path** (December 2025): SoftBank acquired DigitalBridge ($3B), which owns stakes in Switch and other data center operators, but this doesn't provide the same level of control or security assurance

**Significance**: Gap between capital availability ($500B commitment) and secure physical infrastructure to house that capital. The consortium can fund AI development but cannot guarantee fortress-level security for the most sensitive applications.
