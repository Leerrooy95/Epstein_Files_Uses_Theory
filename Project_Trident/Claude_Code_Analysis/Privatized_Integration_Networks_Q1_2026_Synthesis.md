**Key Finding**: The same network of actors appears across all four domains: Saudi PIF, UAE MGX, Oracle, Silver Lake, and individuals including Jared Kushner and Yakir Gabay. This is not a claim of coordination—it is an observation that the same entities hold positions of influence across multiple systems simultaneously.

## Conclusion: Why This Matters

This research documents a structural shift in how international relations, territorial governance, and capital allocation operate. The pattern shows:

1. **Privatization of Diplomacy**: The Board of Peace creates a pay-to-play alternative to UN mechanisms, with permanent membership purchasable and authority centralized in a single individual.

2. **Financialization of Territory**: "New Gaza" treats post-conflict reconstruction as a real estate development opportunity, with housing supply calibrated far below population need.

3. **Capital Integration Without Normalization**: Gulf sovereign wealth now profits from Israeli settlement infrastructure via US private equity intermediaries, creating economic integration without requiring diplomatic normalization.

4. **Technical Military Integration**: MEAD-CDOC enables functional defense cooperation between states that lack formal diplomatic relations, using CENTCOM as a bridge.

5. **Constitutional Incompatibility**: Democratic states with strong constitutional frameworks (Italy, Germany, France) have documented their inability to participate in structures that concentrate authority in a single foreign leader.

The question is not whether this pattern exists—the evidence is documented. The question is what it means for the international order that these mechanisms are now operational.
