#### 1. Government Changes and China Policy Shifts: Country-by-Country Breakdown

##### Australia (2022)
The 2022 federal election represented a pivotal shift from conservative-led policies under the Liberal-National Coalition, which had strained relations with China through trade disputes and security hawkishness. Labor's victory under Anthony Albanese prioritized diplomatic stabilization, leading to rapid engagement.

- **Election and Leadership**: On May 21, 2022, the Australian Labor Party secured a majority, with Albanese sworn in as Prime Minister on May 23, replacing Scott Morrison's conservative government. This ended nine years of coalition rule, with Labor emphasizing pragmatic foreign policy.
- **Policy Shifts**: Initial rhetoric focused on "stabilizing" ties, contrasting Morrison's confrontational stance. Trade restrictions imposed by China (affecting $20 billion in exports) were lifted progressively: coal in February 2023, barley in August 2023, and wine reviews leading to full removal by March 2024. Diplomatic visits included Albanese's meeting with Xi Jinping at the G20 in November 2022 (first in years) and a state visit to China in November 2023.
- **Timeline to Engagement**: Engagement initiated within 3-6 weeks post-election (e.g., June 2022 ministerial meetings), with full pivots (trade lifts, visits) unfolding over 6-18 months. Rhetoric evolved from caution to mutual benefit emphasis by late 2022.

##### UK (2024)
The 2024 general election ousted the long-ruling Conservatives, who had adopted a hawkish China policy amid security concerns. Labour under Keir Starmer pursued a balanced reset, navigating US alliances and economic needs.

- **Election and Leadership**: On July 4, 2024, Labour won a landslide (412 seats), with Starmer becoming Prime Minister on July 5, replacing Rishi Sunak's Conservatives. This marked the first Labour government in 14 years, promising consistent foreign policy.
- **Policy Shifts**: Adopted a "cooperate, compete, challenge" framework, softening rhetoric from prior "threat" designations. A relations audit was initiated in May 2025 (released June), recommending engagement. Diplomatic efforts included Starmer's calls for stable ties by December 2025, amid warnings on Taiwan but focus on self-interest.
- **Timeline to Engagement**: Initial resets in late 2024 (4-5 months post-election), with audits and rhetoric changes by mid-2025 (10-11 months). Visits and trade talks intensified by late 2025.

##### Canada (2025)
The 2025 election occurred amid Liberal leadership change, with Mark Carney replacing Justin Trudeau. Liberals retained power in a minority, fending off Conservative gains, and pivoted to China amid US trade pressures.

- **Election and Leadership**: On April 28, 2025, Liberals won 155 seats (minority), with Carney as Prime Minister, continuing centre-left rule but with fresh leadership amid conservative polling leads. This was framed as Liberals "replacing" potential Conservative dominance.
- **Policy Shifts**: Rhetoric emphasized "recalibrating" relations to reduce US dependence, especially post-Trump tariffs. Trade talks focused on energy and agriculture; Carney's state visit to China (January 13-18, 2026) secured agreements.
- **Timeline to Engagement**: Signals in November 2025 (7 months post-election), with visit in January 2026 (9 months). Shifts driven by US trade wars.
