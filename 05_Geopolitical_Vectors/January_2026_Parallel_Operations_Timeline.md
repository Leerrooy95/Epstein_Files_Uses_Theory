## Part III: Saudi Counter-Offensive (Yemen)

### Background: The December 2025 STC Offensive

In December 2025, the UAE-backed Southern Transitional Council (STC) launched "Operation Promising Future," rapidly seizing territory across southern Yemen including:
- Hadhramaut governorate
- Al-Mahra governorate  
- Key oil infrastructure and ports

This expansion toward Saudi Arabia's border triggered Riyadh's response.

### Saudi Response Timeline

**December 29-30, 2025**:
- Saudi-led coalition conducted airstrikes on UAE vessels at Mukalla port
- Coalition stated vessels carried UAE-linked weapons for STC
- Saudi Arabia publicly accused UAE of "undermining its national security"

**January 1, 2026**:
- Yemen declared 90-day state of emergency
- Yemen ordered all UAE forces to withdraw within 24 hours
- UAE announced full military withdrawal from Yemen

**January 2-4, 2026**:
- Saudi-backed "National Shield" forces launched counter-offensive
- Over 350 Saudi airstrikes reported on STC positions
- PLC forces retook Hadhramaut and Al-Mahra governorates

**January 7, 2026**:
- Saudi-backed forces entered Aden (STC capital)
- STC leader Aidarous al-Zubaidi removed from Presidential Leadership Council
- Al-Zubaidi charged with "high treason"
- Al-Zubaidi fled to UAE via Somaliland (Saudi officials stated UAE "smuggled" him out)

**January 9, 2026**:
- STC members in Riyadh announced dissolution of the council
- Dissolution disputed by STC officials in Aden and Abu Dhabi
- Saudi Defense Minister Khalid bin Salman called it a "courageous step"

**Source**: Wikipedia "Southern Transitional Council"; Al Jazeera; Middle East Eye; Arab News; CNN; Washington Post; Soufan Center

## Part IV: The Saudi-UAE Rupture

### Significance

The January 2026 events marked a fundamental shift in Gulf relations:

**Before January 2026**:
- Saudi Arabia and UAE were coalition partners in Yemen (since 2015)
- Both were major US security partners
- Both participated in overlapping investment networks (Stargate, TikTok deal)

**After January 2026**:
- Saudi Arabia publicly accused UAE of threatening its national security
- UAE military expelled from Yemen
- UAE-backed political entity (STC) dissolved
- Analysts describe relationship as "diplomatic divorce"

### Broader Disputes Revealed

The Yemen rupture exposed tensions across multiple theaters:

| Region | Saudi Position | UAE Position |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Yemen** | Backs central government (PLC) | Backed separatist STC |
| **Sudan** | Backs Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) | Backs Rapid Support Forces (RSF) |
| **Somalia** | Opposes Somaliland recognition | Supports Somaliland |
| **Israel** | Conditional normalization | Abraham Accords signatory |

### Analyst Assessments

The European Council on Foreign Relations noted: "Developments in Somalia, Sudan and Yemen have triggered Saudi Arabia's remarkable diplomatic and military reinvestment in the Red Sea region, as the country aims to rally an anti-UAE-Israel regional alliance."

The Christian Science Monitor reported analysts calling it a "diplomatic divorce" between the two powers.
